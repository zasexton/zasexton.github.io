— GENERAL NOTES —

A portfolio for work at the intersection of physics, computation, and healthcare.

Read sequentially. Each numbered note states an assumption that informs the work shown on the following sheets.

  1. Models support reasoning. Computational models are tools for thinking about coupled systems — not a substitute for understanding them.
  2. Multiphysics is the default. Real biomedical environments couple flow, heat, mechanics, and transport. The interesting questions live at the seams.
  3. Reproducibility is structural. Assumptions, parameters, and outputs are versioned and inspectable so studies can be reused, not rebuilt.
  4. Visuals reveal physics. A good visualization makes model behavior, sensitivity, and tradeoffs easier to evaluate without abstracting away what they mean.
